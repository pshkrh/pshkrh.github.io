A short review of the book - no spoilers (kind of)!

#The Review

My experience with Origin was a very strange one. I had actually pre-ordered the hardcover version from Amazon about 3 months before it released.

I got it (I think) on release day, and it sat in my bookshelf for more than a year.

I've actually read quite a few of Dan Brown's books before and I generally enjoy them a lot - Deception Point is possibly my favourite of them all.

A month ago, I finally decided to start reading the book. In the year plus that had passed, quite a few friends had already finished reading the book. Sadly, I couldn't discuss it with them even though I'd had it for the longest!

So once I picked up the book, needless to say I was quite excited. 

The initial story, although a bit slow to set up, was interesting and the premise of Edmond Kirsch's presentation was very enticing. It was a fresh breath of air to have some technology involved in a Robert Langdon story - those generally are very history focussed and I know next to nothing about it.

Once the stage was set, and the story started to speed up, it was a very enjoyable read. I couldn't keep the book down and I wanted to know what would happen next, where Langdon would end up, how the multiple plot lines going forward would meet at the end.

All that said, the ending was sadly, in my opinion, underwhelming. The first part of Kirsch's presentation was pretty good, the second one, not so much. Had the presentation order been flipped and the first part been hyped throughout the entirety of the book, I think I would have had a much more satisfying experience.

It also doesn't help that one of the other plot lines didn't really lead anywhere, and although it was very nice, wasn't really adding much to the story except give one of the (somewhat important) side characters some more depth.

Overall though, the book was quite solid, and I'd give it a 4/5.

It was hard not to describe things because I didn't want to spoil anything so the review was a bit short, but this was also my first time writing about a book like this.

Until next time!
